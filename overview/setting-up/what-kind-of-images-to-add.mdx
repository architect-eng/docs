**1. Hero Images (2+)**

- Use soft or subtly textured backgrounds that allow text overlays to remain legible.
- Choose images that convey the overall tone or emotion of the product, service, or message.
- Avoid overly busy visuals or high-contrast patterns that distract from key headlines.

**2. Product or Core Offering Visuals (5+)**

- Include clear, high-resolution visuals of the product, app interface, or main service.
- Focus on visuals that communicate value or demonstrate use cases.
- Ensure consistency in lighting, perspective, and colour treatment for a cohesive look.

**3. Supporting Visuals (3+)**

- Add graphics, illustrations, or comparison visuals that help explain key concepts or features.
- Examples: side-by-side comparisons, process diagrams, or benefit breakdowns.

To help us give our agent the right context to build your agentic page(s) for your team, we need a few materials to get started. This ensures the page is accurate, within the scope of your brand, and engaging for your prospects.

Again, all of the requests noted below are not *required* immediately but the more context you can provide, the more knowledgable your agent gets.

Below is the step-by-step flow of what to provide and how.

---

### **Request #1: 1-5 of Your Best Performing Landing Pages**

- Export your **existing landing page(s)** into a **PDF format**.
    - This gives us a baseline for messaging, structure, and design.
- If you have multiple versions (e.g., product-specific or feature-based pages), include as well

---

### **Request #2: Include Product Documentation / Proof Points / Case Studies**

- Send us any **relevant product docs** that explain your offering in detail.
- These could include:
    - Product one-pagers
    - Technical overviews
    - Case studies or customer one-pagers
    - Testimonials or customer quotes
    - Awards, certifications, or third-party recognitions
    - Setup / implementation guides
- The more detail you provide, the better our agent can build informative content.

---

### **Request #3: Include Sales Materials**

- Share materials you’d normally send to a prospect in-funnel, such as:
    - Pitch decks
    - Case studies
    - Sales brochures
- These assets help ensure the Agentic Page speaks to your audience’s pain points and objections.

---

### **Request #4: Provide Marketing Content & Competitor Context**

- Gather any **marketing materials** that capture your brand’s voice, such as:
    - Campaign assets
    - Blog posts
    - Ad creatives
    - Examples of competitor landing pages you **like** (for inspiration)
    - Competitor messaging you **don’t want to copy** (to differentiate)
    - Notes on your **unique positioning** in the market

---

### **Request #5: Share Brand Assets**

- Provide **brand guidelines** and **visual assets** such as:
    - Logo files (SVG or high-res PNG preferred)
    - Color palettes
    - Fonts
- These will help us design the page in a way that feels authentically yours.

---

### **Request #6: Package & Send Materials to Architect Team via Slack / Email**

- Collect all files into a single folder (Google Drive, Dropbox, or ZIP file).
- Send the folder link to your Architect point of contact.
- ✅ **Tip**: Make sure link permissions are set so our team can access the files.

---

## ✅ Summary of What to Provide

- **1-5 Landing page examples** (PDF)
- **Product / Proof Points / Case Studies docs**
- **Sales materials** (case studies, decks, brochures)
- **Marketing & Competitor content**
- **Brand assets** (logos, fonts, colors)
