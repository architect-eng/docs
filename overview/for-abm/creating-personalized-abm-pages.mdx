## Step 1: Choose Your Granularity

First, decide how personalized you want the page to be. The level of granularity determines how specific the copy and messaging will be for the visitor.

**Your Options:**

1. **By Company Name:** Best for targeting specific accounts where you don't know the exact visitor, but you know the organization (e.g., "Welcome, [Company Name] Team").
2. **By Job Title:** Best for targeting specific roles across various companies (e.g., tailored messaging specifically for a "VP of Sales").
3. **By Full Name (Most Extensive):** The highest level of personalization. The page is built for one specific human (e.g., "Hi [First Name], here is a custom Loom workflow for [Company]").

---

## Step 2: Prepare Your Lead Data (CSV)

Once you have chosen your granularity, prepare a `.csv` file containing your leads. The columns required change based on the option you selected in Step 1.

### Option A: If targeting by **Company Name**

*Required Columns:*

- `Company Name`
- `Company URL`

> **Example Row:**`Acme Corp`, `www.acmecorp.com`
> 

### Option B: If targeting by **Job Title**

*Required Columns:*

- `Job Title`
- `Company Name`
- `Company URL`

> **Example Row:** `Director of Customer Support`, `Acme Corp`, `www.acmecorp.com`
> 

### Option C: If targeting by **Full Name** (Deepest Personalization)

*Required Columns:*

- `Full Name`
- `Company Name`
- `Job Title`
- `Company URL`

> **Example Row:**`Jordan Lee`, `Acme Corp`,  `VP of Sales`,  `www.acmecorp.com`
> 

---

### Optional Fields:

The agent will automatically create an SEO Title, Description and Page Link based on the rest of the information and create the page in English. 

*Can be added to each row:*

- `Language`  (e.g., EN, ES, FR)
- `Buyer Persona`  (e.g., Sales Leader, Support Manager)
- `SEO Title`  (e.g., Architect)
- `SEO Description`  (e.g, Personalise Every Visit)
- `Page Link`  (URL of the final page e.g., tryarchitect.com/jordan-lee)

> **Example Row :**`Jordan Lee`, `Acme Corp`,  `VP of Sales`,  `www.acmecorp.com` , `ES` , `Sales and Marketing`
>

## Step 3: Provide Context to the Agent

This is the "brain" of the operation. You must provide a clear short brief so the AI understands who we are targeting, whether this is a warm lead or a new lead, and the overall goal.

**What to include:**

1. **Campaign Context:** Who are you targeting and what is the goal? Is this a warm or a cold lead? 
2. **Design/Language Guard Rails (Optional):** Are there specific words to use or avoid (e.g., framing the product as a "companion" rather than a "replacement")? Are there structural rules to follow (e.g., keeping copy extremely concise to avoid wall-of-text fatigue)?
3. **Buyer Persona Context (Optional):** If you have buyer personas you are targeting include information on this here. 

### 📝 Best Practice Example

*Below is an example of a context brief for a campaign where Loom (asynchronous video) is targeting heavy Slack users to reduce meeting fatigue.*

> 
> 
> 
> **Campaign Context**
> 
> This is a LinkedIn Campaign targeting cold leads who are experiencing "notification overload" and "meeting fatigue" but are not yet using asynchronous video. They have had a briefing on the company but this is the first time they have seen the product.
> 
> **Design/Language Guard Rails**
> 
> - Never position Loom as a "replacement" for Slack; always refer to it as a "companion" or "integration."
> - Do not assume the user knows how easy it is to record a video. Avoid technical jargon and assure them that recording does not require a studio setup or a script.
> - Keep the copy extremely concise. Because we are solving for "wall of text" fatigue, the campaign itself must avoid long, dense paragraphs.

---

## Step 4: Define the Call to Action (CTA)

Finally, tell us where the user should go next. This should be a specific action and a destination URL, or a Form Fill. You can set this on the specific page group settings. 

**Example A (Product Signup):**

- **Text:** "Install Loom for Chrome"
- **URL:** `https://www.loom.com/signup`
